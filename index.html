About Me

I'm a data analyst with nearly a year of professional experience and a lifetime of learning and growing outside of my comfort zone. I'm always keeping myself busy and looking for my next project - a new recipe, a plant on clearance at Lowe's that needs rescuing, a new data certification, or a mountain I haven't yet climbed. Keep reading to see what I've accomplished so far!

My drive for higher education began in high school where I enrolled in the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme in my hometown of Allen, TX (yes, the one with the $60 million football stadium). I then moved onto graduate from the University of Houston in December 2022 with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematical Biology with a minor in Medicine & Society. In these years, I focused on Python in 2 different experiences: an undergraduate research experience where I wrote a program simulating electrical heart spiral waves, and an evolutionary biology lab where I used the language and many external packages to investigate fish genome changes as a result of the warming climate.

Then, I enrolled at UTHealth Houston School of Public Health to pursue a Master of Science in Biostatistics. Here, I directed a food pantry for students and staff. I also began working for Brighter Bites, a national non-profit that distributes produce to families, as a Graduate Research Assistant. I used STATA to make reports detailing program growth for stakeholders and grant writers. After graduation, I began working for Brighter Bites as a full-time Data Analyst. This role was similar to my previous one, but also included Power BI and SQL for more complicated queries and comprehensive dashboards. Unfortunately, I was laid off from this role in December 2025 due to the government's budget cuts of SNAP-Ed. Despite this, I still greatly value impact-driven work and hope to continue giving back to my community, whether through my occupation or volunteer experience.
